## What Is Smart DNS Proxy, Actually?

Think of it like this: normally when you visit a website, your ISP's DNS server basically tells the whole internet where you're located. Geo-restrictions use that info to block you. Smart DNS Proxy intercepts those DNS queries and routes them through servers in the target country — so as far as Netflix or Hulu is concerned, you're sitting in their living room.

The clever part? Unlike a VPN, Smart DNS isn't encrypting all your traffic. Your actual streaming data goes straight to you at full speed. This is why people who've tried both often stick with Smart DNS for everyday streaming — it doesn't slow you down.

Smart DNS Proxy takes this a step further by bundling a full VPN into the same subscription at no extra cost. So if you do need encryption — say you're on public Wi-Fi at an airport — you can switch to VPN mode. Two tools, one price.

The service covers **400+ streaming channels across 44 countries**: Disney+, Hulu, Amazon Prime Video, BBC iPlayer, ESPN, NFL Game Pass, Pandora, DAZN, and hundreds more. Setup runs across pretty much every device — Windows, Mac, iPhone, Android, Samsung and LG smart TVs, PlayStation, Xbox, Roku, Apple TV, and routers.
